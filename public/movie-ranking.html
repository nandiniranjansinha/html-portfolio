The best movies according to Nandini

My top three movies of all time

  1. Zindagi Na Milegi Dobara

    2. "Zindagi Na Milegi Dobara" is a Bollywood film released in 2011. Directed by Zoya Akhtar, the movie follows the journey of three best friends who embark on a road trip to Spain as part of a pact made years ago.

    The story revolves around Kabir (played by Abhay Deol), Imran (played by Farhan Akhtar), and Arjun (played by Hrithik Roshan), who have different personalities and priorities. Kabir is about to get married and wants to have one last adventure before settling down. Imran is a writer who is stuck in a rut and seeks inspiration for his work. Arjun is a wealthy investment banker who is struggling with his personal and professional life.

    The friends set off on a road trip across Spain, where they engage in various thrilling activities, including skydiving, deep-sea diving, and the famous La Tomatina festival. As they navigate these experiences, they confront their fears, confrontations, and personal demons. Along the way, they also meet Laila (played by Katrina Kaif) and Natasha (played by Kalki Koechlin), who bring a romantic angle to the story.

    During the trip, Kabir realizes that he is not truly happy with his impending marriage and has feelings for Natasha. Imran discovers his passion for poetry and learns to let go of his inhibitions. Arjun confronts his strained relationship with his father and realizes the importance of love and friendship.

    "Zindagi Na Milegi Dobara" explores themes of friendship, love, self-discovery, and living life to the fullest. It encourages the audience to embrace the present moment and overcome their fears and limitations to experience the true essence of life.

  2. The Sky is Pink

    3. "The Sky Is Pink" is a Bollywood film released in 2019, directed by Shonali Bose. The movie is inspired by the true story of Aisha Chaudhary, a young girl who battled a life-threatening illness called pulmonary fibrosis.

    The story is narrated from the perspective of Aisha (played by Zaira Wasim), who recounts the journey of her parents Aditi (played by Priyanka Chopra) and Niren (played by Farhan Akhtar) and their unconventional yet deeply loving relationship. Aditi and Niren's lives change when they learn that their daughter, Aisha, has a serious medical condition.

    Despite the challenges they face, Aditi and Niren are determined to provide Aisha with a happy and fulfilling life. They become her pillars of strength, supporting her medical treatments and ensuring she experiences joy and adventure. The family's bond strengthens as they navigate through the ups and downs of Aisha's health.

    The film also delves into the emotional struggles faced by Aditi and Niren, who try to balance the care of their sick daughter with the needs of their son, Ishaan (played by Rohit Saraf). It showcases their unwavering love for Aisha and their determination to make every moment count.

    "The Sky Is Pink" explores themes of love, family, resilience, and the celebration of life in the face of adversity. It depicts the power of human relationships and the impact they can have on overcoming challenges. The film aims to inspire audiences to cherish the precious moments of life and find hope and strength in difficult circumstances.

  3. Dil Dhadakne Do

    4. "Dil Dhadakne Do" is a Bollywood film released in 2015. Directed by Zoya Akhtar, the movie revolves around the dysfunctional Mehra family as they embark on a luxurious cruise trip to celebrate their parents' 30th wedding anniversary. The film explores the complexities of their relationships, societal pressures, and personal aspirations.

    The Mehra family is headed by Kamal Mehra (played by Anil Kapoor) and his wife Neelam (played by Shefali Shah). They have two children, Ayesha (played by Priyanka Chopra) and Kabir (played by Ranveer Singh). Kamal and Neelam face financial troubles with their failing business, and their marriage is strained due to Kamal's infidelity.

    During the cruise, Ayesha, who is unhappily married to Manav (played by Rahul Bose), develops feelings for Sunny (played by Farhan Akhtar), a journalist she meets on board. Kabir, on the other hand, dreams of becoming a pilot but is pressured by his father to take over the family business.

    As the voyage progresses, the Mehra family's facade of harmony starts to crumble, and their hidden secrets and desires come to the surface. The film delves into themes of societal expectations, familial obligations, and individual freedom.

    "Dil Dhadakne Do" combines elements of drama and comedy to explore the dynamics of a wealthy Indian family, their struggles, and their journey towards self-discovery and acceptance.
